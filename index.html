A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man Of India

Here's a time line of A.P.J abdul kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931, to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island
  • 1954 - he graduated in physics from university of Madras.
  • 1955 - He moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in madras institute of technology.
  • 1960 - graduated from madras institure of technology.
  • 1969 - Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980
  • 1970s - Kalam also directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme.
  • 1980s - His research and educational leadership brought him great laurels and prestige in the 1980s, which prompted the government to initiate an advanced missile programme under his directorship.
  • 1992 - appointed as Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.
  • 1997 - Kalam received India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 2002 - he won the presendential election and served the country from 2002 to 2007
  • 2015 - kalam died on july 27,2015.
